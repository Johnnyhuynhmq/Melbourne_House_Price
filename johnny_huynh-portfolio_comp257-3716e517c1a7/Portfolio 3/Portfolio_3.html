Online Retail Customer Clustering¶

The goal of the exercise is to use clustering techniques to idenfity customer segments in the data that might be suitable for targetted marketing or market research. There is a discussion of this along with an example analysis (in R) in this blog post. Your task is to reproduce this in Python and comment on the results that you observe.

Your Analysis¶

The main purpose of this clustering is discovering high and low value customer segments for marketing purposes.

For simplicity and more efficient computation, K-means clustering algorithm will be used in the analysis. In case of such a large dataset (4338 rows), K-means will return the results much faster than other clustering algorithms (e.g. Hirerachical) due to not having to compute distance matrix for each pair of data points.

The number of pre-defined clusters (k) will be trials and errors, whatever sensible and explainable number of k will be accepted (arbitrary). However, let's make a scatter plot to get an holistic view of relationships among Value, Count and Recency.

# Define K-means clustering model for k clusters

def k_means_alg(k):
    clf = KMeans(init='k-means++', n_clusters=k)
    k_features = ['Count_Log', 'Value_Log', 'Recency_Std']
    clf.fit(customers[k_features])
    labels = clf.predict(customers[k_features])
    customers['Cluster'+str(k)] = labels
#Scatter plot for Log of Frequency and Log of Value, with cluster as a categorizer.
    colour = ['r', 'g', 'b', 'y', 'black']
    for i in range(k):
        plt.scatter(customers['Count_Log'][customers['Cluster'+str(k)]==i], customers['Value_Log'][customers['Cluster'+str(k)]==i], 
                    c=colour[i], label='Cluster '+str(i))

    plt.legend()
    plt.xlabel('Log Frequency')
    plt.ylabel('Log Value')
    plt.title('k-Means with ' + str(k) + ' clusters')
    plt.show()

#Summary table in orginal scale
    summary_table_org = pd.DataFrame(clf.cluster_centers_)
    summary_table_org.columns = ['Frequency','Value', 'Recency']
    summary_table_org.index.name = 'Cluster' 
    summary_table = np.exp(summary_table_org[['Frequency','Value']])
    summary_table['Recency'] = (summary_table_org[['Recency']] * np.std(customers['recency'])) + np.mean(customers['recency'])
    print(summary_table)

This model clearly explains the segments:
Very loyal customers (cluster 4), come usually and recently, but buy daily products - which are low in values (makes sense). Therefore, we should not focus on marketing to this group (they will come anyway). Instead, implementing loyalty program to keep them with the business (4th priority)
Loyal customers (cluster 0) who also come usually and recently, but buy more valued products than the first group - therefore the most priority group - need to be paid attention on.
Unusual but valued customers (cluster 1) who do not come usually and recently, but buy really high valued products. This group should be the second priority to marketing to in order to promote more usual purchases.
New customers (cluster 3) who come quite recently, but not frequently and buy medium valued products. This is the third priority.
 Zero-value customers (cluster 2) - have not visited the store for a long time , have not visited the store many times and have not bought valued products. No marketing to this group.

As we move beyond 5 clusters, the segments become less distinguished and less explainable. Therefore, our final recommendation is to employ the 5 clusters model for marketing and promotion strategies.
